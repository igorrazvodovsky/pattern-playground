# Conviviality: standalone quality or distribute?

Analysis of whether `src/stories/qualities/Conviviality.mdx` should remain a standalone quality or be distributed between Agency and Learnability.

## The gap Conviviality names

No existing quality owns the question *"what happens to the person's independent capability over time?"*

- Agency owns present-moment distribution of control
- Learnability owns acquisition of competence *with the system*
- Adaptation owns the system's responsiveness to context
- Conviviality asks what persists *when the tool is absent*

## How dimensions map to existing qualities

| Conviviality dimension | Current home | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Skill transparency | Learnability (domain learning) | As a learning mechanism |
| Progressive withdrawal | Agency (adaptive scaffolding), Learnability (scaffolding) | As a control/support mechanism |
| Transferable understanding | Learnability (extended learnability) | Implied but not named |
| Anti-patterns (silent correction, fluency without foundation) | Agency ("preserving expertise"), Conversation ("skill atrophy"), Adaptation ("skill preservation") | One-liners, not developed |

## Arguments for standalone

1. Names something the others can't — a positive design goal with its own dimensions, heuristics, and anti-patterns, not just a defensive bullet
2. Distinct evaluative question: "Can the person do this without the tool after six months?"
3. Novel, generative anti-patterns: "fluency without foundation" and "silent correction" have no natural home elsewhere
4. Distinct intellectual lineage (Illich, Vygotsky, Ingold) from Agency's HCI/CSCW or Learnability's usability engineering

## Arguments for distributing

1. Zero inbound edges in the pattern graph — no other pattern references it
2. Dimensions decompose cleanly: skill transparency → Learnability, progressive withdrawal → Agency's adaptive scaffolding, transferable understanding → Learnability's extended dimension
3. More essayistic than operational compared to other qualities; "toward convivial patterns" section is explicitly speculative
4. Borderline between a design quality and a value judgement — "convivial autocomplete" is a stretch compared to "accessible form"

## Recommendation: distribute, but deliberately

### 1. Agency: add a "Skill trajectory" section

Expand the existing "Preserving expertise" and "Cognitive engagement paradox" bullets into a proper section. Absorb:
- The core evaluative question ("what does sustained use do to the person?")
- Anti-patterns: silent correction, fluency without foundation, permanent scaffolding
- The six-month heuristic

This is where the *system design concerns* live, because they're about how agency distribution affects the person longitudinally.

### 2. Learnability: add "Transferable understanding" dimension

The interface/domain distinction already gets close. Add a dimension for understanding that persists beyond the tool. Absorb:
- Skill transparency
- Progressive withdrawal (shared with Agency's adaptive scaffolding)
- The "graceful absence" test

### 3. Preserve the Illich framing as reference

Both Agency and Learnability can cite Illich in their expanded sections. The intellectual contribution doesn't need its own page to be present.

### 4. What distribution loses

The unified articulation — a single page where someone reads the complete case for why deskilling matters and how to design against it. If that keeps being needed as a whole, reinstate as standalone.

## Signals to revisit

- If "toward convivial patterns" directions mature into concrete patterns, they'll need a quality to hang from
- If other patterns start needing to reference the deskilling concern as a unit rather than as separate Agency/Learnability aspects
- If the evaluative heuristic ("six months without the tool") proves useful enough to warrant its own page

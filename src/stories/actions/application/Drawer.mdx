# Drawer

A surface that slides in from an edge, letting the actor inspect or act on secondary content while maintaining awareness of the primary task they intend to return to.

The key distinction from [dialog](../?path=/docs/actions-application-dialog--docs) is temporal: a dialog demands resolution before the actor can continue, while a drawer assumes the primary context remains the actor's anchor. The distinction from navigating to a separate page is spatial: the drawer keeps the origin visible (or at least preservable), signalling that this is a detour, not a destination.


## Position variants

<Story of={ DrawerStories.RightDrawer } />

Right drawers are the most common — they follow the reading direction and leave the primary navigation visible.

<Story of={ DrawerStories.LeftDrawer } />

Left drawers typically serve navigation or filter panels, where the content relates to *where to go* rather than *what to inspect*.

## Modal vs. non-modal

A drawer can operate in either mode depending on context:

*Non-modal* (side peek) — the actor can interact with both the drawer and the underlying page. Preferred on wide viewports where both surfaces fit comfortably. This is the drawer's natural state.

*Modal* — the drawer covers enough of the viewport that the background is effectively blocked, often with a scrim. On narrow viewports this happens on its own; on wider ones it can be a deliberate choice for focused editing.

The transition between these is often viewport-driven rather than explicit: the same drawer component that sits alongside content on a desktop becomes a modal sheet on a phone.

## Design considerations

- *Return expectation*: the drawer's content should be completable or dismissible — if the task is complex enough to become the primary focus, it probably belongs on its own page.
- *Stacking*: opening a drawer from within a drawer is possible but rapidly increases cognitive load. If the secondary content needs its own secondary content, the information architecture may need rethinking.
- *Persistence*: some drawers persist across navigation (e.g., a chat panel). These are closer to persistent [modes](../?path=/docs/foundations-modality--docs) than transient ones — the design considerations shift accordingly.
